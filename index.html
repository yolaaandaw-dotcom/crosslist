Onboarding · How GSD works

Things MIT students consistently get blindsided by.

Read this before week one. The deck's "MIT orientation session" condensed into four cards.

01

Critique ≠ presentation

You'll pin work on a wall and stand next to it while 3–6 faculty + outside critics talk about it for 15–25 minutes. They will interrupt you. They will disagree with each other. You don't need to "win" — you need to defend your reasoning. Silence is fine; bullshit is not.

02

"Studio" means a 4-hour block

A studio meets twice a week for 3–4 hours, plus expected independent work — usually 12–20 hrs/week total. Closer to UROP intensity than to a problem-set class. Do not stack two studios.

03

Deliverables are physical

Final review usually means: large-format prints (A1 minimum), a physical model (often built that week), and a digital file package. Plan model-shop time early — the queue gets brutal at finals.

04

Grading is qualitative

Most studios grade Distinction / Pass / Low Pass / Fail. There are no point totals. Faculty assess: development across the semester, defensibility of decisions, quality of execution. Show your iterations — finished work alone reads as suspicious.
